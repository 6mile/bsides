10 am - Ronnie Islam

"DevSecOps: Rich Data, Poor Insights"

Modern software delivery is a complex challenge for security. Security as a enabler is expected to provide @scale service. How to aggregate & corelate data from change management, software delivery and security services to drive actionable insights to achieve reasonable Secure Outcome (software delivery and maintain infrastructure).


10.35 am - Shelly Mills & Kat McCrabb

"Crisis Averted: Planning, Practicing, Prevailing"

Get equipped to face any cyber crisis by Planning, Practicing and Prevailing. This talk dives into the essentials of Incident Response Plans, playbooks, and tabletop exercises, showing how preparation and practice can turn chaos into controlled, confident action. Learn how to build and refine your organisation's incident resilience, ensuring you're ready for whatever comes your way.


11.05 am - Break


11.20 am - Tim Kiermaier

"Quantum Risk: A Measurement of the Coming Probabilities"

As quantum computing rapidly advances, its potential to disrupt traditional cryptographic systems presents significant risks to global cybersecurity. This presentation examines the critical threats posed by quantum computing, focusing on the breakdown of current cryptographic ecosystems. We will delve into the technical timelines required to implement effective countermeasures and strategies for adapting to the evolving risk landscape.

1.15 pm - Joel Panther

'"Stop spying on me!" Blocking adversarial DNS requests.'

Adversarial and advertising DNS traffic pose threats to security and user privacy. This talk discusses strategies for identifying and blocking unwanted DNS requests, leveraging DNS blackholes.


1.50 pm - Artem Brunov

"Hacking AS400"

We will explore the world of the IBM AS/400, now known as the IBM iSeries. Despite being considered an old-school system, the AS/400 remains a critical component in many organizations, particularly within financial institutions for handling essential data. This talk is based on a true story of hacking into an AS/400 system, uncovering its security mechanisms, and revealing potential vulnerabilities.


2.25 pm - Adriana Moreno

"Maximizing Cyber Defense: The Strategic Advantage of XDR Platforms"

They will learn about the core features of XDR, including its ability to integrate and centralize data from various security tools, detect advanced threats across multiple vectors, and automate response actions. Through real-world case studies, participants will see the tangible benefits of XDR in reducing incident response times, improving threat detection accuracy, and providing comprehensive visibility across their IT environment. Additionally, they will gain insights into evaluating whether XDR is the right fit for their specific business needs and how to approach its implementation to maximize ROI and scalability.


3.00 pm - Coffee Break


3.30 pm - Arijit Paul

"Threat Hunting AWS CloudTrail Logs with Microsoft Sentinel: Real-Time Attack Demo"

Unlock the secrets of cloud security with a live demo on threat hunting in AWS CloudTrail logs using Microsoft Sentinel. Witness an attack exploiting a misconfigured server, and learn to develop KQL-based detection rules to safeguard your cloud environment.


4.05 pm - Colby Prior

"Running a company CTF"

This year I am running a CTF internally at my company. This talk will go over the whole process of running your own CTF and show you how you can do the same.


4.40 pm - Thomas Roccia

"YaraToolkit: Your Yara Swiss Army Knife"

So you want to hunt malware like a pro? You heard about YARA but never took the time to learn it properly? What if you could wield a tool that does the heavy lifting for you? YARA is the most used tool by threat researchers and malware analysts; it can be used for identifying malware patterns and tracking threat actors. In this talk, I will discuss YARA and how to use it. I will go through the basics and more advanced use cases, then introduce YaraToolkit.
